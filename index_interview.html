Our offer

What led us down this path

  • Health Samurai developed and implemented ONC certified EHR in three California hospitals
  • We were doing everything from research and development to user support ourselves
  • Hospitals didn’t hire a single person to help us. So we were getting all the feedback firsthand
  • We saw all the diversity of user requirements and understood that it is just not possible to satisfy all of them with a single monolithic EHR
  • That’s why we chose to build a platform which will allow to build a myriad of apps on top of it to satisfy all the needs

Gaps in Meaningful use requirements

  • Meaningful use concern about processes and not the outcomes
  • Meaningful use requirements are not aligned with hospital goals
  • Meaningful use program is welcomed by many CEO and CFO, less by CNO, physicians and nurses
  • A lot of public organizations are not ready to receive public measure reporting data

Why is it bad?

Why not implement both Meaningful use and important hospital requirements?


  • You cannot implement everything, you have to choose
  • Right now hospitals and physician offices are forced to choose meaningful use requirements and have to delay a lot of other things

Security Measures

  • AWS hosting is HIPAA compliant
  • No protected health information (PHI) stored on local devices
  • Data-at-Rest is encrypted, SSL encryption of Data-in-Transit
  • Biometric authentication of Users when applicable
  • Comprehensive audit log and monitoring tools
  • We are educating our users. Security measures built into a product have to be supplemented by an organization culture and policies.

Interoperability?

  • All our solutions are built around HL7 FHIR
  • which stands for Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources
  • ...and called sometimes the "HTML" of healthcare
  • FHIR has well documented mapping to other medical standards such as HL7 v2, HL7 v3 or CDA
  • You can get clinical data from other vendors’ software into the FHIR storage with minimal efforts
