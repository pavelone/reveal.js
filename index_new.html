Solve your interoperability problems

Solve your interoperability problems

Let's look at a specific case...


ACO (Accountable Care Organization) uniting multiple hospitals with several EHRs from different vendors, other apps and patient owned devices gathering data for physicians


  • How will you connect all this together?
  • How will you provide your physicians with access to clinical data from all EHRs, patient devices etc?

Health Samurai solution

Health Samurai offers to gather all the data into a centralized FHIR server and build modern web and mobile applications on top of it

Why centralized FHIR server?


  • You really need to bring all the data into one place. Having data spread out thru multiple databases will make it very hard/impossible to query combinations of data from different sources
  • FHIR server and FHIR related tools are the tools which fit very well for solving interoperability problems

Why centralized FHIR server?


  • fhir has well documented mapping with hl7 v.2
  • fhir has well documented mapping with CDA
  • fhir is getting support from all major EHR vendors (see Project Argonaut)
  • fhir is the best medical standard

Create an application targeting a large market which will supplement EHR system from multiple vendors

Supplement multiple EHR

Let's look at a specific case...


The pharma company wants to provide hospitals with CDS module to use with their drugs or... someone wants to bring an advanced patient portal/engagement tools to the community uniting multiple hospitals


  • How will you integrate with all the EHRs?
  • What else?

Health Samurai solution

Health Samurai offers to gather relevant clinical data into the FHIR server. This data will be synchronized with the data within EHR by means of HL7, CDA or custom API.

Health Samurai solution


Health Samurai will develop a modern web application which will be started from the EHR (using its API) allowing physicians switch from the EHR into the new application and back transparently. Documents created within the new web application will be stored back to the EHR database if needed.

  • fhir offers a clinical model from the best industry experts thus it is a great fit for storing application clinical data
  • fhir has well documented mapping with hl7 v.2

  • fhir is getting support from all major EHR vendors (see Project Argonaut)
  • fhir is the best medical standard
